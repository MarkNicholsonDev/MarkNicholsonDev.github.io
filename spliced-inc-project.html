Summary of Project

During my two and a half years on this project, I worked as an Associate Systems Design Engineer (Technical Designer) in the systems team. The team focused on prototyping and implementing new game mechanics under direction from game designers and the provided design documentation. I owned several systems across the project, including several minigames which were distinct gameplay experiences and systems that players could interact with within the game world.

Workflow and Responsibilities

Features were implemented primarily in Angelscript(a typed scripting language similar to C#) alongside C++ and Blueprint. Much of my work was improving legacy implementations and polishing for things like multi-platform support to allow for players playing across different platforms within the same game instance to have a similar experience.

The extent of my ownership was of the full technical implementation for these features, along with responsibility for regular playtesting of my content to ensure a high quality stable experience. I often worked on legacy systems that had been passed from other programmers and also created new systems myself from game design documentation.

My work often extended past pure gameplay scripting to contributing to other parts of the gameplay experience such as prototype UI implemented with Common UI and UE5's sequencer for creating cinematics.

Retrospective on Spliced

Since I joined mid-development many systems were already in place, often in a initial prototype form but needed significant improvements e.g. improvements to game feel, polish and platform parity across all supported platforms. I particularly enjoyed working on these legacy systems and making sure these features aligned with the creative vision/intended design from the designer and the documentation and ensuring a better overall quality across the project. It was especially satisfying bring these left behind systems up to the same standard as the rest of the project. That often involved technically challenging refactoring work, which made the end result infinitely more rewarding.

The role involved me doing significant amount of cross-departmental coordination with Art, UI, Animation and Engineering. This often for asset requests or discussing technical or performance considerations for the features and their code implementations which is something I quickly became comfortable with.
